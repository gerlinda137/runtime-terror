# Sprint 3: Contact us page and refactor

- **What was done:** Refactored the Angular CLI naming for component generation to follow the latest convention. Added a Contact Us component with a form. Also wrote a Contact service for future interaction with the backend.
- **Problems:** How to rename the existing files without creating a bunch of conflicts while my teammates are still working on them. After submitting the form and resetting it, all the fields got marked as errors, and I had to figure out how to fix it.
- **Solutions:** Turned out that after submit Angular marks the form as `submitted`, and Material shows errors on invalid fields whenever the form is submitted — so my empty fields lit up red even after `form.reset()`, because reset clears the values but not the submitted flag. The fix was to reset through the form's directive instead: `formDir.resetForm()` clears both the values and the submitted state. The form directive is what Angular attaches to `<form [formGroup]>` — it links the HTML form to my reactive form and tracks its state (like whether it was submitted).
- **What I learned:** What a form directive is, and how to write services for talking to the backend.
- **Plans:** Add interaction with a real server, and work on other components later.
- **Time spent:** 8 hours
